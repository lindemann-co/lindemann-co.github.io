Motivation

I recently developed hypersensitivity to light. Here I share some measures and equipment that help me to live as normal a daily life as possible. If you or someone close to you is going through the same situation, I hope these few lines will be useful to you.

Context

In May 2023, I had an autoimmune reaction which temporarily rendered me completely blind. Fortunately, I was remarkably taken care of by the teams in CHUV and the Hôpital Ophthalmique Jules Gonin in Lausanne. Less than two months after the end of the treatment, I had recovered most of the sensory abilities that constitute vision:

  • visual acuity (the famous “10/10 for each eye”),
  • the visual field,
  • the perception of colors, starting with yellow and blue and therefore letting red come last, which is not without irony for a Swiss resident.

Today, only my sensitivity to light and sensitivity to low contrasts are still deficient. Concretely, I am much more easily dazzled than before by white, clear objects and saturated colors (the neon lights of stores, for example). Poor light management also makes it harder for me to spot tone-on-tone objects, such as my black phone on a black surface.
